FictionFilter

MasseyHacks VI Project by Eric Andrechek, Jainil Shah, and Rabab Azeem



Why a fact checker?

Manually fact-checking articles and posts takes time and man-power. With more and more content created online every day, the potential dangers we face with the spread of misinformation is every-growing. That is why we created FictionFilter, an ML-based service to find misinformation and false claims in articles fully-autonomously.

How does this all work?

The article credibility checker uses Keras and previously labeled articles to train a LSTM (long short term memory) model which is a type of a RNN (recurrent neural network). LSTM models are very useful for classifying articles since they are 4-layered neural networks with loops in them and thus, allow information to persist, unlike normal neural networks. The raw text is encoded using one-hot encoding which is then applied to the LSTM model that classifies it as credible or fake.
